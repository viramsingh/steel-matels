The process begins with the procurement of raw materials, primarily iron ore, coal, and limestone, which are essential for steel production.

Stainless steel

+

Iron ore is smelted in a blast furnace to produce molten iron. This iron is then processed further to remove impurities, resulting in molten steel.

Alloy

+

Several methods can be used for steelmaking, including the Basic Oxygen Furnace (BOF) and Electric Arc Furnace (EAF) processes. In BOF, oxygen is blown into the molten iron to remove impurities and adjust the composition.

steel

+

The molten steel is cast into various shapes and forms, such as billets, slabs, or ingots, depending on the desired end product.

components

+

Heat treatment processes, such as annealing, tempering, and quenching, may be applied to enhance the mechanical properties and durability of the steel.

Fixing & Support

+

Steel products may undergo surface finishing processes, such as coating, galvanizing, or painting, to protect against corrosion and improve aesthetics.
